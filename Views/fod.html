Project Motivation

The Grainger’s museum is a repository of collections documenting the life and career of Percy Grainger, who is an artist, musician, and educator, with extremely wide interests and expertise. The museum has a major part exhibiting Percy’s collections, and about a half part used for temporary exhibitions. The temporary exhibition is usually based on a topic that somehow related to Percy Grainger himself, and usually has been used for a promotion to attract more visitors to the museum.

Investigation Methods

We interviewed the Grainger Museum visitors and conduct interviews about their experience for their visit, and also interviewed museum staffs and volunteers who work there, to find out what they think this museum needs and don't needs.

We've spent a week visiting the museum and observe the visitors and staffs without disturbing them because we want to avoid some disconnection between what our interviewees words and their behaviours.

We also visited other galleries and museums in Melbourne, to see what those museums have done on novel interaction techniques so we can come up something new and even better.


Key Findings & Analysis

For visitors, we found that they have very different ages & preferences, which means this museums attracts people from all the different ages, which is great. Also, most of them found the museum is good enough, only some first-commers think they could use some tour guidance, which usually provided by one of the staffs on the counter.

While for staffs and volunteers, the most frequent comments we gathered is that they're hard to get effective feedbacks from visitors. The museum has a visitor book for visitors to leave comments and signatures, from which we found quite a lot comments on it and most of them are positive. What bothers them is the feedback system on an ipad that they have to hand to visitors and watch them fill it all.


Visitor Personas

Paper Prototype

This video demonstrate our design for the museum, a web-based tour guidance(i.e. No need to download an app), which can be accessed by WIFI, hyperlink or scan a QR code.

Panorama photos are provided, showing museum exhibitions and areas, each of the exhibits will have brief context intro, both in text and audio. Visitors can leave comments and feedbacks using their own devices, or choose to complete the post-tour questionnaire on the museum's website.
