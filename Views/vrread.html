Stage 1
Motivation, Related work, Gap

Motivation

  • Tired of holding a thick book: What if books can float themselves? An actual book can not, but a virtual book definitely can!

  • Virtual reading is very common in big screens, but in reality, VR has been widely used in education and entertaiment, but not in reading.

  • 'Henry' by Oculus: an interactive book character.

  • Awesome work done by Mike Alger, who made an VR interface that almost allow VR to be an substitute for PCs.

    • Related work

    Reading has been always a very natural way of people obsorbing knowledge. We found that there have been a few studies focus on the virtual environment reading, they explored ways of presenting reading content, and media such as sounds effects that can be used for virtual reading.

    During reading, a single media effect brings less disturbing experience than multi-media effects. The multi-media effects should be carefully designed and tested for its reading experience.

    Among paging, scrolling and RSVP, while RSVP can be very effective acting as a prompt for notification, paging and scrolling remain as the most common ways of reading, which bring better reading experience and comprehension of content.

    With related studies that provide relevant guidelines, this project, therefore, will have those guidelines for the initial design and implementation.
